The solar system includes the Sun and all the objects that orbit it, such as eight planets, dwarf planets, moons, asteroids, and comets. The eight planets are divided into the four inner, rocky terrestrial planets (Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars) and the four outer, gas or ice giants (Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune). All of these celestial bodies are held together by the Sun's gravity and are located in the Orion Arm of the Milky Way galaxy.
